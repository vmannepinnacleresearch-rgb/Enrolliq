The Industry Problem

Patient recruitment has a
data infrastructure gap.

Published research consistently identifies the same root causes behind enrollment failures. The technology exists to solve them — it just hasn't been applied to this layer of the problem yet.

🗑️
Recruitment status history is not preserved
Industry-standard CTMS platforms store a patient's current status — not their journey. Every transition is overwritten. The analytical value of how patients move through the funnel is permanently lost, making conversion analysis and process improvement nearly impossible.
Source: Documented across clinical CTMS architecture reviews
👤
No unified patient identity across studies
A patient referred across two studies at the same site exists as two unconnected records. Prior contact history, eligibility notes, and interaction context are invisible to the team managing the second referral. Sites lose institutional knowledge with every handoff.
Source: Common across multi-study site network operations
📐
Inconsistent workflows prevent benchmarking
When sites define the same status differently, data becomes incomparable across a network. Sponsors and CROs cannot identify what's working, which sites outperform, or where process improvements would have the most impact.
Source: Tufts CSDD multi-site performance research
📊
Enrollment visibility arrives too late for sponsors
By the time sponsors and CROs receive site enrollment reports, the data is already weeks old. Studies that are falling behind are identified after the window for easy intervention has passed — costing time, budget, and patient access to trials.
Source: Applied Clinical Trials, Tufts CSDD enrollment performance studies

How It Works

Designed so doing the work
produces the intelligence.

The core principle: a coordinator logs an interaction and the analytics update automatically. No separate reporting step. No manual exports. The data builds itself.

01 / CAPTURE
🎯

One Patient. Every Study.

Every lead enters as a universal patient record — not a study-specific record. When a patient is referred to a second or third study, the full prior history is immediately visible. No duplicate records. No lost context. No starting from scratch.

02 / TRACK
📋

Every Transition Preserved.

Every status change is a timestamped event in an append-only record. Nothing is overwritten. Sponsors and operations teams can see conversion rates, time-in-stage, and drop-off points going back to day one — because the history was never deleted.

03 / FORECAST
🔮

Intelligence Before It's Asked For.

Enrollment forecasts, site benchmarks, referral ROI, and speed metrics update continuously. Sponsors see risk before it becomes a missed milestone. Sites see exactly what they need to do. Everyone operates on the same live data.

Full Feature Set

Built for every role.
Useful from day one.

Every feature serves two purposes: reducing coordinator burden and generating better data for sponsors, CROs, and operations teams — simultaneously.

🔮
Enrollment Forecasting

AI-powered projections based on each site's actual historical conversion rates. Sponsors know months in advance whether a study is on track — not weeks after it falls behind.

👤
Universal Patient Profile

One record per patient across all studies and all sites. Full interaction history, visit log, and complete status timeline — preserved forever, never overwritten.

📊
Live Funnel Dashboards

Real-time sponsor and site director views into every recruitment stage, filterable by site, study, date, referral source, and coordinator. Always current.

🔗
CTMS Integration Layer

Open API designed to connect with existing CTMS platforms. Enrolliq handles the recruitment and pre-enrollment layer — compatible with the clinical systems sites already use.

📡
Referral Source Intelligence

Full conversion and ROI breakdown by every referral source — not just lead volume. Sponsors and site directors see which channels produce enrolled patients, not just inquiries.

⚡
Speed & Response Metrics

Time-to-first-contact, contact-to-screen velocity, no-show rates. Identify where patients disengage before they drop out of the funnel entirely.

🧪
Configurable Pre-Screener

Study-specific eligibility questionnaires built by coordinators or operations teams. Every outcome — pass, fail, reason — is automatically logged and included in sponsor analytics.

💬
Integrated Communications

SMS and email sent directly from the patient profile. Every message auto-logged to the interaction timeline — full communication history for every patient, always accessible.

🏥
Multi-Site Network Control

Network admins see everything. Site directors see their sites. Coordinators see their patients. Sponsor views configured per study. Role-based access that matches real org structures.

Data Engineer Clinical Research Pipeline Architecture Healthcare Data Product Builder

I work in clinical research data. I got curious about why the enrollment problem was so persistent — and realized it was an architectural issue nobody had approached from a data-first perspective.

I'm a Data Engineer with a background in building ETL pipelines, analytics layers, and reporting infrastructure across healthcare. Working in clinical research data gave me a close view of the operational side of recruitment — specifically the gap between the data that exists inside these systems and the data that sponsors, site directors, and operations teams actually need to make timely decisions.

The patterns I kept encountering are well-documented across industry research: status history that gets overwritten, patient records that don't persist across studies, and workflows that vary enough between sites to make cross-network benchmarking nearly impossible. These are architectural problems — and architectural problems have architectural solutions.

That's what I'm building with Enrolliq. A recruitment intelligence layer designed from first principles: append-only history, universal patient identity, standardized workflows, and live analytics that emerge from the work rather than requiring a separate reporting effort.

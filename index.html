The Industry Problem

Patient recruitment has a
data infrastructure gap.

Published research consistently identifies the same root causes behind enrollment failures. The technology to address them exists — it just hasn't been applied to this layer of the problem.

🗑️
Recruitment status history is not preserved
Standard CTMS platforms store a patient's current status — not their journey. Every transition overwrites the last. The analytical value of how patients move through the funnel is permanently lost.
Source: Documented across clinical CTMS architecture literature
👤
No unified patient identity across studies
A patient referred to two studies at the same site exists as two disconnected records. Prior contact history and eligibility context are invisible on the second referral. Sites lose institutional knowledge at every handoff.
Source: Common pattern in multi-study site network operations
📐
Inconsistent workflows prevent benchmarking
When sites define the same status differently, data becomes incomparable across a network. Sponsors cannot identify what's working or where process improvement would have the most impact.
Source: Tufts CSDD multi-site performance research
📊
Sponsor visibility arrives too late to act
By the time sponsors receive enrollment reports, the data is weeks old. Studies falling behind are identified after the easy intervention window has passed — costing time, budget, and patient access.
Source: Applied Clinical Trials, Tufts CSDD enrollment studies

How It Works

Doing the work produces the intelligence.

The core principle: a coordinator logs one interaction and analytics update automatically. No separate reporting step. No manual export. The data builds itself.

01 / CAPTURE
🎯

One Patient. Every Study.

Every lead enters as a universal patient record — not a study-specific record. Prior interaction history, eligibility notes, and contact context are immediately visible when a patient is referred to a second study.

02 / TRACK
📋

Every Transition Preserved.

Every status change is a timestamped event in an append-only record. Nothing is overwritten. Sponsors can see conversion rates, time-in-stage, and drop-off patterns going back to day one — because the history was never deleted.

03 / FORECAST
🔮

Intelligence Before It's Needed.

Enrollment forecasts, site benchmarks, referral ROI, and speed metrics update continuously. Sponsors see risk before it becomes a missed milestone. Sites see exactly what they need to act on. Everyone operates on the same live data.

I work in clinical research data. I got curious about why the enrollment problem was so persistent — and realized it was an architectural issue nobody had approached from a data-first perspective.

I'm a Data Engineer with a background in building ETL pipelines, analytics layers, and reporting infrastructure across healthcare. Working in clinical research data gave me a close view of the operational side of recruitment — specifically the gap between the data that exists inside these systems and what sponsors, site directors, and operations teams actually need to make timely decisions.

The patterns I encountered are well-documented across industry research: status history that gets overwritten, patient records that don't persist across studies, and workflows that vary between sites in ways that make cross-network benchmarking nearly impossible. These are architectural problems. Architectural problems have architectural solutions.

That's what I'm building. A recruitment intelligence layer designed from first principles — append-only history, universal patient identity, standardized workflows, and live analytics that emerge from the work rather than requiring a separate reporting effort. Phase 1 is clinical trial recruitment. Phase 2 is the broader healthcare problem this architecture was always capable of solving.
